# CodeAlpha_AI_Impact_on_Jobs_2030
Exploratory Data Analysis of AI's impact on jobs, salaries, automation risk, and workforce trends for 2030 using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.

# AI Impact on Jobs 2030 – Exploratory Data Analysis

## Project Overview

This project explores the potential impact of Artificial Intelligence on jobs by analyzing factors such as salary, education level, work experience, AI exposure, automation probability, and job risk categories. The objective is to identify patterns, trends, and insights that can help understand how AI may influence the future workforce by 2030.

## Dataset

The dataset contains 3,000 records with information on:

* Job Titles
* Average Salary
* Education Level
* Years of Experience
* AI Exposure Index
* Automation Probability
* Risk Categories
* Various skill-related metrics

## Objectives

* Perform data cleaning and quality checks.
* Analyze salary distribution across different job roles and education levels.
* Identify occupations with the highest AI exposure.
* Examine automation risks across industries.
* Explore relationships between experience, salary, and AI impact.
* Generate meaningful visualizations and business insights.

## Tools & Libraries Used

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook

## Key Findings

* Salaries are distributed across a wide range of job roles.
* Education level has an influence on salary, with higher qualifications generally associated with higher earnings.
* Several occupations show high AI exposure, indicating significant AI influence on job tasks.
* Automation probability varies across professions, highlighting differences in future job security.
* Experience remains an important factor affecting salary levels.

## Project Structure

* Data Cleaning
* Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
* Data Visualization
* Insights & Conclusions

## Conclusion

The analysis provides valuable insights into how AI may reshape the workforce by 2030. Understanding AI exposure and automation risks can help professionals, students, and organizations prepare for future career and skill requirements.
